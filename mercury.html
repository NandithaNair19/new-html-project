MERCURY

Mercury is the closest planet to the Sun and the smallest in our solar system, with a surface that closely resembles Earth's Moon, featuring craters
and vast plains. Despite its proximity to the Sun, Mercury experiences extreme temperature variations, with daytime temperatures soaring
above 800°F (427°C) and nighttime temperatures plummeting to nearly -330°F (-201°C). Mercury has no atmosphere to retain heat, which causes these dramatic temperature swings.

Here are few features:

Feature Description
Distance from the Sun 57.9 million kilometers
Diameter 4,879 kilometers
Orbital Period 88 Earth days
Day Length 59 Earth days
Temperature Daytime: 430°C, Nighttime: -180°C
Moons None
Atmosphere Oxygen,sodium,hydrogen,helium,potassium
Color Gray, rocky surface
